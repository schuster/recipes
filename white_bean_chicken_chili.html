White Bean Chicken Chili

Serves 4

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced medium
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 1 (15-ounce) can Great Northern beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (15-ounce) can cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 (4.5-ounce) can green chiles, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 ½ teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • ⅛ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth (NOTE: try an extra 1 or 2 cups of broth next time; the soup seems to not have enough liquid sometimes and serves less than I'd expect)
  • 2 tablespoons corn starch, for thickening (optional)
  • Shredded Monterey Jack cheese, for garnish

Directions

  1. Heat the oil over medium-high heat in a large pot. Add the onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion softens and starts to turn translucent.
  2. Add the chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through.
  3. Add the beans, chiles, garlic, cumin, pepper, cayenne pepper, and broth and bring to a boil. To thicken (optional), mix the corn starch with 2 tablespoons water and shake in a tightly-sealed container. Slowly stir the mixture into to the pot until chili has reached desired thickness.
  4. Serve in bowls with Monterey Jack cheese sprinkled on top.
