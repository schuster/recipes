Laziji (Sichuan Fried Chicken)

Source: Milk Street Kitchen: https://www.177milkstreet.com/tv/chicken-the-chinese-way

Ingredients for seasoning salt:

  • 3 Tbsp whole sichuan peppercorns,
  • 2 tsp sugar, divided (2 tsp for seasoning salt, 2 Tbsp for marinade, 2 Tbsp for dredge)
  • 1 tsp Kosher salt, and 2 tsp for dredge

Ingredients for chicken/marinade:

  • 1/3 cup soy
  • 3 Tbsp rice vinegar
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 egg whites
  • 2 lbs chicken thighs, chopped into 1 inch pieces

Ingredients for chili oil:

  • 1 oz (about 1 cup) of whole Sichuan chiles (arbol chiles also work well)
  • 3 Tbsp Sichuan chile flakes
  • 1 Tbsp whole Sichuan peppercorns
  • 1 cup vegetable oil or other neutral-flavored oil

Ingredients for dredge and frying:

  • 2 cups cornstarch
  • ¼ cup ground Sichuan peppercorns
  • 2 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp Kosher salt
  • 2 quarts peanut oil or other neutral-flavored, high-smoke-point oil

Ingredients for dressing:

  • 8 scallions, chopped
  • 1 cup whole cilantro leaves

Instructions

  1. For the seasoning salt, toast peppercorns in a pan over medium-low heat. Grind the peppercorns and strain them to remove inedible parts. Add sugar and salt, then set aside.
  2. Mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, 2 Tbsp sugar, and egg whites. Pour over the chicken thighs, then let sit for 20–30 minutes at room temperature.
  3. For the chili oil, mix together the Sichuan chiles, Sichuan chile flakes, 1 Tbsp whole Sichuan peppercorns, and 1 cup of vegetable oil. Heat the oil over medium-low heat until it reaches 275 degrees, about 3–5 minutes, then strain the oil.
  4. Drain the oil and other moisture off of the chicken in a large strainer or colander. In a large bowl or container, mix together the cornstarch, ¼ cup ground Sichuan peppercorns, 2 Tbsp sugar, and salt. Dredge the pieces of chicken a few at a time then shake off excess coating in a fine-mesh strainer. Place chicken on top of a wire cooling rack.
  5. In a large Dutch oven, heat the peanut oil to 350 degrees. When the oil has reached temperature, fry the chicken in batches until crisp with golden highlights, about 5 minutes per batch. Transfer chicken to wire cooling rack. Be sure to let the oil come back up to 350 degrees before starting the second batch.
  6. Put the fried chicken in a large bowl and add scallions, 1 Tbsp of seasoning salt, 6 Tbsp of chili oil (or to taste), and cilantro. Serve with extra chili oil on the side.
