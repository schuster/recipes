Corned Beef and Cabbage

Source: (adapted from) America's Test Kitchen

Serves 8–10

Ingredients:

  • 4.5–5 pounds corned beef
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon black peppercorns
  • 6 carrots, peeled, halved crosswise, thick ends halved lengthwise
  • 1.5 pounds small red potatoes, unpeeled
  • 1 head green cabbage (2 pounds), uncored, cut into 8 wedges
  1. Adjust oven rack to middle position and heat oven to 275°. Rinse corned beef and pat dry with paper towels. Cut 8-inch square triple thickness of cheesecloth. Place remaining 3 garlic cloves, 2 bay leaves, and 1 tablespoon black peppercorns in center of cheesecloth and tie into bundle with kitchen twine. Place corned beef, spice bundle, and 2 quarts water in Dutch oven. (Corned beef may not lie flat but will shrink slightly as it cooks.)
  2. Bring to simmer over high heat, cover, and transfer to oven. Cook until fork inserted into thickest part of brisket slides in and out with ease, 2.5–3 hours.
  3. Remove pot from oven and turn off oven. Transfer corned beef to large ovensafe platter, ladle 1 cup of cooking liquid over meat, cover, and return to oven to keep warm.
  4. FOR THE VEGETABLES: Add carrots and potatoes to pot and bring to simmer over high heat. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until vegetables begin to soften, 7 to 10 minutes.
  5. Add cabbage to pot, increase heat to high, and return to simmer. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer until all vegetables are tender, 12 to 15 minutes.
  6. While vegetables cook, transfer beef to cutting board and slice ¼-inch thick against grain. Return beef to platter. Using slotted spoon, transfer vegetables to platter with beef. Moisten with additional broth and serve.
