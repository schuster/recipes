Curried Butternut Squash Soup

Serves 8-12

Source: New Boston Globe Cookbook

Ingredients

  • 3 large peeled butternut squash, cut in half lengthwise
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 large Spanish onion, coarsely chopped
  • 2 Cortland apples, peeled, cored, and coarsely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • Pinch of cayenne pepper, or to taste
  • 7 cups chicken stock

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 475 °F. Place the squash, cut side up, on a rimmed baking sheet. Sprinkly sparingly with oil, salt, and black pepper. Cover with foil, shiny side down, and transfer to the hot oven. Roast for 25–30 minutes, until the squash becomes soft.
  2. In a large flameproof casserole, melt the butter. Cook the onions and apples over medium heat, stirring often, or for eight minutes, until they soften.
  3. Add the curry, cayenne, and ginger. Cook, stirring constantly, for two minutes.
  4. Use a spoon to transfer soft pieces of squash to the pot. Cook, stirring, for two minutes.
  5. Pour in the stock and bring to a boil. Set the cover on askewed and cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, for 20 minutes.
  6. Turn the heat to low and puree the soup with a stick blender. If you don't have a stick blender, puree the soup in a normal blender in batches.
  7. Return to medium heat. Taste for seasoning and add more salt and cayenne pepper if you like.
